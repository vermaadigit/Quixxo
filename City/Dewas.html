BRIEF HISTORY OF DEWAS

The history of Dewas district is intertwined with its twin treaty States, established by Tukoji Rao(I) and Krishnaji Rao in the 18th century. Facing challenges from Pindaris and neighboring powers, the fortunes of both branches fluctuated until merging into Madhya Bharat in 1948. Notable rulers like Tukoji Rao III, educated at prestigious institutions, played crucial roles, marked by titles like His Highness and Raja. Dewas witnessed significant events during the Indian War of Independence in 1857, epitomized by the bravery of figures like Thakur Dowlat Singh of Raghogarh, who faced the British with courage but was ultimately captured and hanged.

Nemawar, comprising Kannod and Khategaon tahsils, adds to Dewas district's historical tapestry, mentioned in ancient texts as Nabhapur. Over time, it came under the rule of various powers, including the Peshwas, Scindhia, and Holkar, before being integrated into Madhya Bharat in 1948. The district's administrative structure evolved with the formation of tahsils, reflecting changes in governance and territorial divisions, as documented in historical records and official gazettes.
