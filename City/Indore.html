History of Indore

Indore, once a riverside village called Juni Indore, evolved into a pivotal trade center by the late 15th century, owing to its strategic position along the Sarasvati River. It served as a crucial stopover for pilgrims and a military encampment for the Marathas and Mughals. The Zamindars of Kampel fortified the village, laying the groundwork for its growth into a bustling trade hub in central India.

The Holkar dynasty, led by Malhar Rao Holkar, further propelled Indore's development. Malhar Rao established Malharganj and commenced the construction of the illustrious Rajwada palace. Subsequent rulers like Ahilyabai Holkar and Yashwantrao Holkar II contributed to the city's progress, fostering infrastructure projects and forming strategic alliances.
